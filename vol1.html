Part 1
The Paywall Trap

The Anatomy of a Survey Tool Bait-and-Switch

Here is the playbook every major survey platform follows. It's not accidental. It's a business model called the "freemium trap" — and it's been quietly fleecing researchers, students, and entrepreneurs for over a decade.

Step 1: Offer a "free" plan. Make it attractive enough to get you to invest time building your survey. Let you send the link. Let your respondents start answering.

Step 2: Cap the responses. You've been collecting data for three weeks when suddenly — the survey stops. You've hit 25 responses (SurveyMonkey), or 10 per month (Typeform), or 100 total (JotForm). Your momentum is gone. Your participants are confused. Your deadline is looming.

Step 3: Make the upgrade look reasonable. "$39/month? Sure, I need my research." But now you've paid. And you'll pay again next month. And the month after. The trap has closed.

"SurveyMonkey's free plan is almost unusable: 10 questions per survey and 25 responses per survey. That's not a plan — that's a demo." — Common researcher complaint, 2025–2026
